Books of the Month

Welcome to our Books of the Month page, where we showcase a curated selection of captivating reads handpicked by our team of book enthusiasts. Dive into a world of literary wonders as we present you with our top picks for this month, ranging from gripping thrillers to heartwarming tales, thought-provoking non-fiction to enchanting fiction. Each book featured on this page has been carefully selected for its quality, relevance, and ability to captivate readers of all tastes and interests. Whether you're seeking an escape into a new world, an exploration of timely topics, or simply a delightful journey through the pages of a well-crafted story, you're sure to find something to delight and inspire you among our Books of the Month. Explore our selection, discover your next favorite read, and let the magic of storytelling transport you to new heights of imagination and insight.

The Name of the Wind

Patrick Rothfuss
Fantasy

This fantasy novel follows the life of Kvothe, a gifted young musician and magician, as he recounts his journey from a troupe of traveling performers to a renowned arcanist and adventurer. As Kvothe unravels the mysteries of his past and the truth behind legends, he becomes entangled in a web of magic, intrigue, and danger.

The Martian

Andy Weir
Science Fiction

In this science fiction novel, astronaut Mark Watney is stranded alone on Mars after his crew mistakenly leaves him behind during an emergency evacuation. As he struggles to survive in the harsh Martian environment, Watney must rely on his ingenuity, resourcefulness, and determination to signal Earth for rescue.

The Shadow of the Wind

Carlos Ruiz Zafón
Mystery, Historical Fiction

Set in post-World War II Barcelona, this mystery novel follows young Daniel Sempere as he discovers a forgotten book by an obscure author named Julian Carax in the Cemetery of Forgotten Books. As Daniel delves into the mystery surrounding Carax's life and the sinister figure known as "Laín Coubert," he becomes embroiled in a dangerous game of secrets, betrayal, and redemption.

The Nightingale

Kristin Hannah
Historical Fiction

Set in Nazi-occupied France during World War II, this historical fiction novel follows the lives of two sisters, Vianne and Isabelle, as they navigate love, loss, and resilience in the face of unimaginable hardship. As they each struggle to survive and resist the horrors of war in their own ways, their bond is tested and strengthened by the choices they make and the sacrifices they endure.

Educated

Tara Westover
Memoir, Autobiography

This memoir recounts Tara Westover's extraordinary journey from growing up in a strict and isolated survivalist family in rural Idaho to eventually earning a PhD from Cambridge University. As she grapples with the challenges of pursuing an education while confronting her turbulent past and family dynamics, Westover's story is a testament to the power of resilience, self-discovery, and the pursuit of knowledge.

Circe

Madeline Miller
Fantasy, Mythology

Circe is a retelling of the Greek myth of the witch Circe, known for her encounter with Odysseus in Homer's Odyssey. In Miller's novel, Circe's story is explored in depth, from her lonely childhood among the gods to her banishment to the deserted island of Aiaia. As Circe discovers her powers of witchcraft and encounters various mythical beings, including Odysseus himself, she grapples with themes of power, agency, and self-discovery.

Where the Crawdads Sing

Delia Owens
Historical Fiction, Mystery

Set in the marshes of North Carolina, this novel follows the life of Kya Clark, known as the "Marsh Girl," as she grows up isolated from society after being abandoned by her family. As Kya navigates her solitary existence, she forms unexpected connections and confronts prejudice and suspicion from the local community. When a murder investigation brings the outside world to her doorstep, Kya's secrets and resilience are put to the test.

The Underground Railroad

Colson Whitehead
Historical Fiction

This Pulitzer Prize-winning novel reimagines the historical Underground Railroad as an actual network of underground trains and tunnels used by slaves seeking freedom in the antebellum South. The story follows Cora, a young slave on a Georgia plantation, as she escapes her brutal existence and embarks on a perilous journey northward. As Cora encounters various challenges and atrocities along the way, the novel explores themes of freedom, justice, and the legacy of slavery in America.

Station Eleven

Emily St. John Mandel
Science Fiction, Dystopian

Station Eleven is a post-apocalyptic novel that follows the interconnected lives of several characters before and after a devastating flu pandemic wipes out most of humanity. As survivors struggle to rebuild society in the aftermath of the collapse, the novel weaves together narratives of love, loss, art, and resilience. Through its exploration of memory, culture, and the human spirit, Station Eleven offers a haunting and ultimately hopeful vision of a world forever changed.

Little Fires Everywhere

Celeste Ng
Contemporary Fiction, Drama

Little Fires Everywhere is a novel set in the planned community of Shaker Heights, Ohio, where the lives of two families become intertwined in unexpected ways. When Mia Warren, an enigmatic artist, and her daughter Pearl move into the picture-perfect suburb, they disrupt the carefully curated lives of the Richardson family. As secrets are unearthed and tensions rise, the novel explores themes of motherhood, identity, privilege, and the search for belonging.
